\paragraph{Production--Attraction Consistency.}
OD demand matrices are not collections of independent pair-wise counts. 
For an OD matrix $\mathbf{Y}_t \in \mathbb{R}_{+}^{N \times N}$, the row and column marginals have clear transportation meanings:
\begin{equation}
P_{i,t} = \sum_{j=1}^{N} Y_{ij,t}, 
\qquad
A_{j,t} = \sum_{i=1}^{N} Y_{ij,t},
\end{equation}
where $P_{i,t}$ denotes the trip production of origin zone $i$, and $A_{j,t}$ denotes the trip attraction of destination zone $j$. 
These quantities describe the aggregate departure and arrival intensities of zones, and impose a natural conservation structure on OD forecasting.

Given the ZINB predictive distribution, we use its conditional mean as the point demand estimate:
\begin{equation}
\widehat{Y}_{ij,t}
=
\mathbb{E}[Y_{ij,t}]
=
(1-\pi_{ij,t})\mu^{\mathrm{NB}}_{ij,t}.
\end{equation}
The predicted production and attraction are then obtained by marginalizing the predicted OD matrix:
\begin{equation}
\widehat{P}_{i,t} = \sum_{j=1}^{N} \widehat{Y}_{ij,t},
\qquad
\widehat{A}_{j,t} = \sum_{i=1}^{N} \widehat{Y}_{ij,t}.
\end{equation}

To encourage the model to respect the aggregate flow structure of OD demand, we introduce a production--attraction consistency regularizer:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{PA}}
=
\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}
\left|
\log(1+\widehat{P}_{i,t})
-
\log(1+P_{i,t})
\right|
+
\frac{1}{N}\sum_{j=1}^{N}
\left|
\log(1+\widehat{A}_{j,t})
-
\log(1+A_{j,t})
\right|.
\end{equation}
The logarithmic transformation reduces the dominance of high-volume zones and makes the constraint robust to the heavy-tailed nature of OD demand.

The final training objective is:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}
=
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ZINB}}
+
\lambda_{0}\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{zero}}
+
\lambda_{\mathrm{PA}}\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{PA}}
+
\lambda_{\mathrm{prior}}\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{prior}},
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ZINB}}$ is the negative log-likelihood of the zero-inflated negative binomial distribution, $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{zero}}$ calibrates the predicted zero probability, and $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{prior}}$ regularizes the learned residual correction to the sparsity prior. 
This regularizer couples sparse pair-wise OD predictions through physically meaningful row and column marginals, encouraging the predicted OD matrix to preserve origin-side trip production and destination-side trip attraction.
